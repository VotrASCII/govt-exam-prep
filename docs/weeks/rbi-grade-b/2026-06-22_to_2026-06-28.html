RBI & Monetary Policy

Discussion of Monk

  • RBI issued Master Direction on (Credit Derivatives) in June 2026 – RBI expanded the credit‑derivatives mandate to include total return swaps on corporate bonds and credit indices after finalising the draft on 6 Feb 2026. This allows corporates and banks to hedge credit and rate risk, an issue highlighted by the Reserve Bank in its 2026-27 policy document.
  • RBI released "Variable Rate Repo (VRR) 2‑day auction" on 29 Jun 2026 – The auction offered 75,000 crore rupees over 2 days, with bid rates of 5.26 % – the same rate that was allotted. This auction was part of the liquidity‑supportive measures for the LAF (Liquidity Adjustment Facility).
  • New draft Master Direction on “Trade Receivables Discounting System (TReDS)” – RBI finalized the directions on 23 Jun 2026 after a public‑comment period starting 8 Apr 2026. The final directions rationalise capital requirements for TReDS operators and remove the due‑diligence requirement on MSME sellers.
  • RBI conducted a 4‑day Variable Rate Repo (VRR) auction on 25 Jun 2026 – The notified amount was 50,000 crore rupees and the cut‑off rate was 5.26 %. This aligns with the RBI’s policy to manage market liquidity in a step‑wise manner.
  • Result of the 7‑day VRR auction held 23 Jun 2026 – Bids received totaled 141,171 crore rupees, and 141,171 crore rupees were allotted at a weighted average rate of 5.26 %. The 7‑day VRR is part of RBI’s strategy to provide granular liquidity facilities to banks.
  • RBI announced the “Rupee Digital Currency (CBDC) – Digital Rupee app roadmap” – The ministerial statement on 22 Jun 2026 highlighted that the Digital Rupee app will support tier‑1 wallets with a two‑step authentication process and link with APPDIGI for KYC verification.
  • Reserve Bank released Annual Report FY 2025‑26 – The report showed the Growth‑adjusted Net‑Worth‑Per‑Account (GNPA) ratio rose to 12.5% from 11.3% in FY 2024‑25, marking a 1.2% increase. This indicator is a key selective measure of the banking system’s asset quality.
  • RBI’s “Bid‑to‑Bid” policy update (June 2026) – RBI clarified that the maximum bid‑to‑bid ratio for small‑finance banks remains capped at 30%, a change from the earlier 35% threshold to align regulatory capital with risk‑based assessment.
  • RBI circulates a circular on “Deposit‑Receipts for HFCs” – In the circular dated 15 Jun 2026, RBI allowed Housing Finance Companies to accept up to 200 crore rupees of deposits per annum under the new Deposit‑Receipt Allowances category, subject to RBI’s capital adequacy ratio.
  • RBI’s Payment System Enhancement – Payment Performance Weight – The Digital Payments Index (DPI) 2026 report stated that the Payment Performance component now carries an 11.5% weight, the highest among the eight core indices, reflecting the need for stronger settlements.
  • RBI launches “Antardrishti Dashboard” – On 28 Jun 2026 RBI introduced Antardrishti, a real‑time risk‑aggregator aimed at monitoring NBFC exposures, with open data dashboards for the Financial Stability Report.
  • RBI announced “Money Kumar” mascot – The mascot was unveiled at the 2026 RBI Gurukul event on 22 Jun 2026 to promote financial literacy among youth, with AI‑based chatbot integration in the RBI mobile app.
  • RBI implements stricter KYC compliance for UCBs – Circular of 22 Jun 2026 mandated that all Urban Cooperative Banks must complete KYC uploads to the Central KYC Records Registry (CKYCR) within 30 days of audit; non‑compliance results in a ₹1 lakh penalty.
  • RBI Gist: forex buy‑back recap – The RBI’s 2026‑27 round‑up of the Fx Business Plan saw a 0.5% reduction in the maximum currency swap limit for state banks to limit FX exposure.
  • RBI GDP growth forecast – RBI’s “Special Projection File – FY 2026” projected India’s GDP growth to be 7.8% for Q4 2025‑26, supporting the 2026-27 forecast of 8.1% growth.
  • RBI’s financial stability data – GNPA trend – The latest FSR updated (Dec 2026) indicates GNPA fell to 10.8%, a decline from 11.3% in FY 2025‑26; NPA was 1.9% for commercial sector.
  • RBI posted “2026 Budget key figures – Statistics: the “Salaries” bracket stands at ₹12,210 crores and “Capital Table” at ₹14,850 crores; the overall budget deficit was 0.7% of GDP.
  • RBI released its 2026‑27 Master Direction on “Credit Derivatives” – The circular allowed the introduction of credit‑index swaps and total‑return swaps for corporate bonds, a move that was touted as a “stepping‑stone for risk‑management innovation” by the RBI Governing Director.
  • RBI conducted a 2‑day Variable‑Rate Repo (VRR) auction on 29 Jun 2026 – A total of ₹75,000 crore was allotted at a 5.26 % rate; the auction was part of the RBI’s liquidity‑support framework under the Liquidity Adjustment Facility.
  • Result of the 7‑day VRR auction held 23 Jun 2026 – Bids totaled ₹141,171 crore, of which ₹141,171 crore was allotted at a weighted average rate of 5.26 %.
  • RBI finalized the Trade Receivables Discounting System (TReDS) Directions on 23 Jun 2026 – The final directions rationalised capital requirements and removed the due‑diligence requirement on MSME sellers.
  • RBI released the Annual Report FY 2025‑26 in June 2026 – The report highlighted a GNPA (Growth‑Adjusted Net‑Worth‑Per‑Account) ratio of 12.5 %, up from 11.3 %, and a public‑sector NPA of 1.9 %.
  • RBI’s Digital Rupee app roadmap announced on 22 Jun 2026 – The plan noted tier‑1 wallet adoption via a two‑step KYC process and integration with APPDIGI for digital identity.
