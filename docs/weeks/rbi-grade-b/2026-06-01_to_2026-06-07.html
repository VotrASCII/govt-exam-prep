RBI Grade B · Week 21 · General Awareness

01 Jun – 07 Jun, 2026

01

RBI & Monetary Policy

  • ★ Repo rate unchanged at 5.25 % – The Monetary Policy Committee (MPC) met from 3‑5 June 2026 and voted unanimously to keep the policy repo rate under the Liquidity Adjustment Facility (LAF) at 5.25 % (c‑bank‑rate unchanged). The standing‑deposit facility (SDF) rate remained at 5.00 % and the marginal‑standing‑facility (MSF) and Bank Rate stayed at 5.50 %. This decision is crucial for candidates because the repo rate is the primary monetary‑policy tool and the 5.25 % level has been the benchmark for the past several meetings.
  • ★ Variable‑Rate Repo (VRR) auctions scheduled – RBI announced a 4‑day VRR auction on 8 June 2026 (₹75,000 cr, 4‑day tenor). Earlier, a 2‑day VRR on 3 June (₹50,000 cr) and a 3‑day VRR on 2 June (₹75,000 cr) were also conducted, each with a cut‑off rate of 5.26 %. VRR auctions are used to manage short‑term liquidity and signal the RBI’s stance on market rates.
  • Treasury‑Bill (T‑Bill) auction outcomes – On 3 June 2026, the 91‑day T‑Bill was issued for a notified amount of ₹12,000 cr; the competitive cut‑off yield was 5.5586 % (price 98.6331). The 182‑day and 364‑day bills were also placed at similar yields. T‑Bill yields move in tandem with repo rates and are a key barometer of short‑term market expectations.
  • Floating‑Rate Bond (FRB) 2031 rate – For the half‑year period 7 Jun 2026 – 6 Dec 2026, the FRB 2031 carried a coupon of 6.64 % per annum (base = 3‑day T‑Bill weighted‑average yield + 1 % spread). FRBs are used to hedge interest‑rate risk and are priced relative to Treasury yields.
  • Sovereign Gold Bond (SGB) premature redemption price – The RBI confirmed that the redemption price for SGB 2021‑22 Series‑III (maturing on 8 Jun 2026) will be ₹15,512 per unit, calculated as the simple average of the closing price of 999‑purity gold for 3 business days (3‑5 Jun 2026). SGBs are a retail‑oriented sovereign‑debt instrument linked to gold prices.
  • Deputy Governor re‑appointment – Shri Swaminathan Janakiraman was re‑appointed as RBI Deputy Governor for two years, effective 26 June 2026. The Deputy Governor plays a pivotal role in monetary‑policy formulation and banking‑sector supervision.
  • RBI penalties on compliance lapses – Canara Bank was penalised ₹41.80 lakh for delayed KYC uploads and mis‑classification of in‑operative accounts. Puran Associates Pvt Ltd. faced a ₹3.10 lakh fine for not re‑classifying restructured loans as non‑performing assets. Nagar Sahkari Bank (U.P.) was fined ₹14.25 lakh for NPA mis‑classification and breach of unsecured‑advance ceilings. These penalties underscore the regulator’s emphasis on asset‑quality monitoring and KYC compliance.
  • Tax exemption for FPIs on G‑Sec interest & capital gains – Effective 1 Apr 2026, foreign portfolio investors (FPIs) and foreign institutional investors (FIIs) enjoy zero tax on interest income and both short‑ and long‑term capital gains from Government‑secured (G‑Sec) holdings. Prior to the amendment, interest was taxed at 20 % and capital gains at 30 % (LTCG 12.5 %). The move is intended to deepen the sovereign‑bond market and attract stable foreign capital.
  • Recognition of Sahamati Foundation as SRO‑AA – RBI approved Sahamati Foundation as a Self‑Regulatory Organisation for the Account‑Aggregator ecosystem, further strengthening the open‑banking framework and data‑sharing architecture.
02

Banking & Financial Sector

  • State‑Government‑Securities (SGS) auction totals – On 2 June 2026, the RBI auctioned SGS for a cumulative ₹14,800 cr across 10 states (e.g., Bihar ₹1,600 cr, Gujarat ₹1,500 cr, Maharashtra ₹1,200 cr). Competitive bids were received from primary dealers, with yields ranging from 7.70 % to 7.97 %. State‑bond auctions are a key source of financing for sub‑national governments and influence the overall yield curve.
  • Government‑Security auction result (GS 2036) – The full‑price auction on 5 June 2026 for the 6.94 % GS 2036 received competitive bids of ₹73,726 cr against a notified amount of ₹34,000 cr. The cut‑off price was 99.79 (implicit yield 6.9686 %). This large‑scale issuance helps the government fund its fiscal deficit at a relatively low cost.
  • Under‑writing commission for GS 2036 – Primary dealers received a commission of 0.43 paise per ₹100 face‑value for additional competitive underwriting (ACU), reflecting the cost of providing liquidity in the primary market.
  • RBI’s forward‑looking surveys (May 2026) – The RBI released results of the Urban Consumer Confidence Survey, Rural Consumer Confidence Survey, and the Inflation‑Expectations Survey. While the data are not detailed here, they influence policy‑formulation by capturing household expectations on price stability and income growth.
  • RBI’s gold‑stock clarification – RBI reiterated that its physical gold holdings stand at 880.52 tonnes, dismissing media reports of a gold‑sale. The figure is part of RBI’s balance‑sheet composition and influences public‑perception of sovereign wealth.
  • Liquidity‑adjustment operations (May 2026) – Net injection through repo and reverse‑repo operations swung between +₹89 billion and ‑₹114 billion across the week, indicating active management of short‑term money market conditions.
  • Regulatory direction on deposit‑rate flexibility – RBI invited comments on draft “Interest Rate on Deposits” amendment directions, seeking stakeholder input on greater pricing flexibility for bulk deposits while ensuring uniform disclosure. This reflects the regulator’s move toward market‑driven deposit pricing.
  • SRO‑AA recognition – The approval of Sahamati Foundation as the SRO for the Account Aggregator ecosystem enables a coordinated governance framework for data sharing among banks, fintechs, and third‑party providers.
  • Penalties for non‑compliance – Apart from the banking‑sector penalties listed earlier, RBI also imposed fines on a private firm (Puran Associates) for violations of asset‑classification guidelines, emphasising the regulator’s strict supervisory stance across both banks and NBFCs.
03

Government Schemes & Budget

  • PM‑KISAN cash transfer – Each farmer family receives ₹6,000 per year (₹2,000 in three instalments). By March 2026, 9.44 crore families had benefitted, accounting for over 25 % of all beneficiary households. This scheme is a key poverty‑alleviation tool and often appears in GA questions on subsidy programmes.
  • PM‑FBY (crop‑insurance) claims – By the end of 2025, claim payments under the Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana amounted to ₹1.96 lakh crore, covering more than 4 crore farmers. The scheme underscores the government’s risk‑mitigation approach for agriculture.
  • PM‑KMY (senior‑citizen pension) – Provides ₹3,000 per month to eligible senior citizens (age 60+) who have contributed for 10–40 years. As of Feb 2026, 24.95 lakh farmers had enrolled, illustrating the scheme’s expansive reach.
  • PM‑Ujjwala LPG connections – The nationwide campaign has supplied 10.55 crore LPG connections (as of 14 May 2026), with an additional 22.42 lakh connections released in the current fiscal year, highlighting progress towards universal clean‑fuel access.
  • PM‑AY (Ayushman Bharat) health cards – 43.52 crore families hold AB‑PMJAY cards; women represent ≈ 49 % (21 crore). The scheme’s scale makes it a recurring exam focus.
  • PM‑Surya Ghar (Muft Bijli) Yojana – The government aims to supply free electricity to 75 lakh households by Dec 2026. As of May 2026, 40 lakh households have already benefited, with a total subsidy outlay of > ₹22,750 crore (₹2,743 crore disbursed in May 2026 alone).
  • PM‑Awas (Housing) progress – Total houses allocated 4.15 crore, sanctioned 3.90 crore, and completed 2.99 crore (as of Mar 2026). Financial assistance under the scheme exceeds ₹4.03 lakh crore.
  • PM‑KUSUM solar‑pump rollout – By Dec 2025, 21.77 lakh farmers benefitted, with 10 lakh+ standalone solar pumps installed and 13 lakh+ grid‑connected pumps. The program supports renewable‑energy penetration in agriculture.
  • National Rural Livelihood Mission (NRLM) – The Jan Dhan‑linked Jan Samarth portal has processed ₹3,00,951 crore (≈ ₹2,76,493 crore disbursed) for 49.55 lakh beneficiaries across 269 lending institutions, demonstrating large‑scale financial inclusion.
  • Digital India – BHASHINI VYOMA Challenge – Launched on 2 June 2026, the contest offers prize money up to ₹80 lakh for AI‑first‑voice applications targeting low‑connectivity devices, reflecting the government’s push for indigenous AI solutions.
04

International Affairs & Organizations

  • India‑South Africa Strategic Partnership – Deputy President H.E. Shipokosa Mashatile met President D R Murmu on 2 June 2026; bilateral trade valued at ≈ USD 15.5 billion. Both sides reaffirmed cooperation in the UN, G20, BRICS, IORA and IBSA, and pledged to reconvene the India‑Africa Forum Summit.
  • India‑Oman CEPA – Came into effect 1 June 2026, removing a 5 % MFN duty on marine products (shrimp, fish, cuttlefish) and providing a duty‑free regime for over 90 % of Indian textile exports to Oman. The agreement is part of India’s broader FT‑A push in the Gulf.
  • India‑UK Vision 2035 – UK Foreign Secretary Yvette Cooper met PM Narendra Modi on 4 June 2026, reaffirming the bilateral “India‑UK Vision 2035” roadmap covering trade, technology and climate cooperation.
  • India‑Venezuela energy talks – Acting President Delcy Rodríguez met PM Modi on 4 June 2026, discussing cooperation in energy, critical minerals, technology and health, underscoring India’s outreach to the Global South.
  • India‑Lao upcoming S&T partnership – On 3 June 2026, Union Minister Jitendra Singh and Lao Minister‑President Mario Voigt signed an MoU covering quantum communication, photonics and joint research, building on a 50‑year S&T relationship.
  • India‑Philippines JWGTI – The joint working group met on 5 June 2026 in Manila, with bilateral trade at USD 3.9 billion. Key sectors discussed: film, energy, ICT/AI, pharmaceuticals.
  • India‑Cameroon cross‑border UPI – On 1 June 2026, RBI and NPCI International launched QR‑code based UPI acceptance for Indian travellers in Cambodia (≈ 4.5 million merchants), making Cambodia the 9th country on the UPI network.
  • BRICS Culture Working Group (CWG) – The 2nd CWG meeting in Varanasi (4‑5 June 2026) involved delegates from Brazil, China, Indonesia, Iran, South Africa, UAE and virtual participants from Ethiopia, Egypt, Russia, discussing cultural cooperation and ethical AI in the creative economy.
  • G20 and critical‑minerals observatory – India and the UK jointly launched the Critical‑Minerals Global Supply‑Chain Observatory on 4 June 2026, aimed at monitoring supply‑chain risks for battery‑grade lithium, cobalt and rare‑earths.
05

Economy & Trade

  • Real GDP growth FY 2025‑26 – Provisional figures released by the Ministry of Statistics (MoSPI) show 7.7 % real GDP growth (vs 7.1 % in FY 2024‑25). Q4 (Jan‑Mar 2026) growth was 7.8 % YoY. These strong numbers underpin the claim that India remains the world’s fastest‑growing large economy.
  • Nominal GDP growth – Nominal GDP rose 8.9 % in FY 2025‑26, reaching ₹ 346.36 lakh crore.
  • GVA growth – Real Gross Value Added (GVA) expanded 7.9 %; nominal GVA grew 9.1 %.
  • Sector‑wise performance – Food‑grain production grew 9.6 %, cement production 8.2 %, finished‑steel consumption 10.4 %, natural‑gas consumption ‑2.8 %, commercial‑vehicle sales 19 %, three‑wheelers 26.7 % (all YoY).
  • Merchandise‑exports growth – Overall exports rose 8.3 % in FY 2025‑26; petroleum‑product exports formed the largest share, while services exports grew 11 %.
  • Imports – Overall imports increased 16.2 %, driven largely by capital goods; the import of machinery & equipment rose 24.9 % YoY.
  • Wholesale‑Price Index (WPI) revision – Base year changed from 2011‑12 to 2022‑23; the item basket expanded from 697 to 957, with solar and wind electricity now included under “Electricity”. Methodology shifted to chain‑based formulation and weights based on Gross Value of Output (GVO). Revised WPI series released on 15 June 2026.
  • Power sector statistics – Installed generation capacity reached 500 GW (≈ 53 % from renewables). Non‑fossil‑electricity share stood at 52.57 % (Feb 2026). The government aims to double renewable capacity to 500 GW by 2032.
  • ATF price‑stabilisation fund – The cabinet approved a one‑time ₹10,000 cr interest‑free support to Oil‑Marketing Companies (OMCs) to cap Aviation Turbine Fuel (ATF) prices for scheduled airlines. ATF price surged from ₹60.50/L (Mar 2026) to ₹142/L (May 2026) (≈ 2.5×).
  • Domestic LPG & PNG – As of 4 June 2026, domestic LPG stock‑outs were absent; PNG connections stood at ≈ 8.82 lakh with an additional 2.98 lakh slated for rollout.
  • Steel production – Crude steel output (May 2026) 14.21 MT (+2.9 % YoY); finished steel 13.94 MT (+7.7 % YoY).
  • Marine‑export performance – FY 2025‑26 seafood export value ₹73,890.46 cr (≈ USD 8.45 bn) and volume 19.72 lakh MT, driven largely by Andhra Pradesh’s aquaculture sector.
  • Foreign Portfolio Investor (FPI) holdings in G‑Secs – As on 12 May 2026, FPIs held ₹ 3,75,171 crore (3.34 % of total outstanding government securities).
  • Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) outlook – India’s “World’s most trusted investment destination” narrative was reaffirmed by the Commerce Minister on 4 June 2026, highlighting nine FTAs signed in the last 3‑3½ years covering 38 developed economies.

Awards, Rankings & Appointments

  • National Panchayat Awards 2025 – Held on 3 June 2026, 42 Panchayats were honoured (34 Deen Dayal Upadhyay and 8 Nanaji Deshmukh awards).
  • MY Bharat Guinness World Record – The “Most Users to Take an Online Quiz in One Week” record (390,812 participants) was certified in 2025 and highlighted during the MY Bharat Youth Convention (6 June 2026).
  • National e‑Governance Awards 2026 – Ten Gold projects were selected, including the “Integrated Cyber‑Security Framework for Digital Banking” (Bank of Baroda) and “AI‑enabled Clinical Decision Support (eSanjeevani)”.
  • PM‑SETU cluster launch (Rajasthan) – Launched on 3 June 2026, the Pradhan Mantri Skilling and Employability through upgraded ITIs (PM‑SETU) targets 5 industrial clusters; the first cluster in Bhiwadi focuses on automotive, electronics and green industries.
  • RBI‑appointed Deputy Governor – Shri Swaminathan Janakiraman’s re‑appointment (effective 26 June 2026) was announced via RBI press release on 5 June 2026.
07

Sports / Environment / Science & Technology

  • World Yogasana Championship 2026 – Inaugurated in Ahmedabad (4‑8 June 2026) with 78 participating countries and > 500 athletes. PM Modi declared yoga a potential future Olympic sport.
  • World Environment Day 2026 (5 June) – PM Modi and Union Ministers launched “Ek Ped Maa Ke Naam” tree‑planting drives; over 1 lakh trees were slated for planting nationwide.
  • Flex‑Fuel Vehicle (FFV) launch – On 4 June 2026, Maruti Suzuki unveiled India’s first flex‑fuel passenger vehicle capable of running on ethanol‑petrol blends E20 – E100. If 50 % of new two‑wheelers adopt FFVs, projected farmer income gain is ₹12,403 crore and ethanol demand 311.8 crore L.
  • First Indian‑made QR‑code cross‑border UPI – Launched on 1 June 2026 for transactions in Cambodia, covering 4.5 million merchants.
  • India’s first AI‑driven Nanomedicine platform – A research team at Agharkar Research Institute reported a biodegradable mesoporous silica nanoparticle (MPPM) delivering siRNA against MCL‑1 and Survivin (June 2026), highlighting the country’s progress in targeted cancer therapeutics.
