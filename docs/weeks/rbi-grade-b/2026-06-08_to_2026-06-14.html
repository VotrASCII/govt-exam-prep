RBI Grade B · Week 21 · General Awareness

08 Jun – 14 Jun, 2026

01

RBI & Monetary Policy

  • RBI conducts a capacity‑building workshop for Farmer‑Producer Organisations (FPOs) in Nagpur (12 Jun 2026).

The Nagpur Regional Office, together with the National Bank for Agriculture and Rural Development (NABARD), hosted > 140 participants from banks, FPOs and state agencies. The two‑day event focused on market linkages, digital fraud‑prevention and RBI’s Integrated Ombudsman Scheme.

  • RBI contributes a technical perspective at the NHRC’s “Safeguarding Human Rights against Digital Arrest Scams” (09 Jun 2026).

RBI representatives proposed the use of AI‑driven pattern detection and a single‑government communication channel to flag suspicious transactions, complementing inputs from the National Payments Corporation of India (NPCI) and TRAI.

  • RBI’s Digital Payment Index (DPI) continues to give the highest weight to Payment Performance (2026).

In the latest DPI, the Payment Performance pillar carries 40 % weight, reflecting RBI’s focus on transaction speed, success‑rate and consumer protection. *(★ perennial high‑priority metric)*

  • RBI’s Financial Stability Report (FSR) shows the Gross NPA (GNPA) ratio for banks at 4.5 % as of March 2026.

The FSR notes a gradual decline from 5.2 % in March 2025, attributing improvement to tighter credit‑risk monitoring and the National Credit Registry. *(★ perennial high‑priority metric)*

  • RBI releases the 2026‑27 Monetary Policy Statement (MPS).

The Monetary Policy Committee (MPC) kept the repo rate at 6.50 %, within the 4‑quartile tolerance band of 4 %‑6 % around the 6 % target, and highlighted that inflation is expected to average 4.3 % in FY 2026‑27. *(★ perennial high‑priority metric)*

02

Banking & Financial Sector

  • E‑CLGS (Export Credit Guarantee Scheme) issues 1,06,549 guarantees worth ₹48 484.26 crore (09 Jun 2026).

96 % of the guarantees (by number) and 86 % of the guaranteed amount go to the MSME sector, and Public‑Sector Banks provide 96 % of the guarantees.

  • Credit Guarantee Scheme for Micro‑Finance Institutions‑2.0 (CGSMFI‑2.0) extended to 31 Aug 2026 (10 Jun 2026).

The maximum loan cap for large NBFC‑MFIs rises from ₹300 crore to ₹1 000 crore (overall ceiling 20 % of AUM). The guarantee fee is fixed at 0.50 % p.a. for the first year and the interest‑rate cap is EBLR + 2 % p.a.

  • GeM (Government e‑Marketplace) shows massive growth of MSE participation (09 Jun 2026).

Registered MSEs increased from 2 396 (2016‑17) to > 11.9 lakh; procurement from MSEs rose from ₹69 crore to > ₹8.69 lakh crore. Women‑owned MSEs grew from 268 to > 2.16 lakh.

  • RBI Nagpur’s FPO workshop emphasized digital‑ledger‑based supply‑chain traceability.

Participants were introduced to e‑KYC, blockchain‑enabled transaction logs, and real‑time credit‑rating dashboards provided by RBI’s Credit Information Bureau (CIBIL) interface.

  • RBI’s Integrated Ombudsman Scheme now covers digital‑payment fraud complaints (2026‑27).

The scheme allows consumers to lodge complaints through a single‑window portal with a prescribed 30‑day resolution timeline. *(★ perennial high‑priority metric)*

03

Government Schemes & Budget

  • Pradhan Mantri Surakshit Matritva Abhiyan (PMSMA) celebrates 10 years (09 Jun 2026).

Over 7.50 crore pregnant women have received nine free ANC services each month; the Maternal Mortality Ratio fell from 130/1  lakh (2014‑16) to 87/1  lakh (2022‑24) – a 43‑point decline.

  • PM Jan Dhan Yojana (JDY) reaches 58.16 crore beneficiaries (May 2026).

Total account balance is ₹3.02  lakh crore, underpinning the Financial Inclusion agenda. *(★ perennial high‑priority metric)*

  • Jal Jeevan Mission 2.0 (JJM 2.0) water‑coverage rises to 81.94 % of rural households (June 2026).

The scheme aims for 100 % tap‑water coverage by 2028, with 1.55 crore water‑conservation structures already built.

  • Rural Development “Viksit Bharat GRAMP” receives a ₹1.25 lakh crore outlay (09 Jun 2026).

Interim allocation of ₹95 692.31 crore includes ₹30 000 crore for MGNREGA and state‑wise allocations covering more than 2.80 lakh Gram Panchayats.

  • PM MUDRA Yojana total loan accounts rise to 57 crore (May 2026).

Disbursements to women account for 66 % of the ₹16.88  lakh crore loan portfolio.

  • PM Kisan Samman Nidhi (PM KSN) disburses ₹5 trillion in FY 2025‑26, supporting ~ 12 crore farmers.
  • National Digital Payments Index (DPI) 2026 places UPI at 2 264 crore transactions, valued at ₹29.53  lakh crore.
  • Digital Rupee (e‑Rupee) pilot expands to 12 new cities (2026).

The Central Bank has authorised ₹1 billion worth of tokenised transactions for government‑to‑government payments. *(★ perennial high‑priority metric)*

04

Economy & Trade

  • Pharmaceutical sector valuation reaches ≈ US$ 60 billion (08 Jun 2026).

The industry targets doubling its size in the next 5 years; exports rose from US$ 14 bn (FY 2014‑15) to US$ 31 bn (FY 2025‑26), with a US$ 50 bn target for 2030.

  • Leather‑footwear‑accessories exports total US$ 4.75 billion (FY 2025‑26).

Tamil Nadu contributed US$ 1.59 billion (≈ 37 %). The sector aims for US$ 50 billion (domestic + export) by 2030, a CAGR ≈ 24 % (2025‑30).

  • India’s merchandise export basket (FY 2025‑26) is worth US$ 863 billion.

Petroleum products constitute the largest share, while services account for US$ 421 billion.

  • West Asia fertilizer stocks (07 Jun 2026) stand at 197.56 MT, about 51 % of the national requirement (383.9 MT).
  • India’s CPI for May 2026 records 3.93 % YoY inflation (combined), with Food CPI at 4.78 %.

The lowest‑inflation items were Potato (‑23.71 %) and Motor car & jeep (‑7.19 %); the highest were Silver jewellery (+155.23 %) and Tomato (+48.43 %).

  • Railway safety audit (10 Jun 2026) finds fire‑safety compliance at all stations.

Joint inspections cover electrical installations, HVAC, emergency exits and sprinkler systems, aiming to reduce fire‑related incidents.

  • Zojila Tunnel (Kargil) reaches ≈ 14 km length, cost ₹6 800 crore (09 Jun 2026).

Travel time between Sonamarg and Minamarg is cut from ≈ 2 hrs to ≈ 30 min.

  • India’s public‑capital‑expenditure rose to ₹12.2 lakh crore in FY 2026‑27 (up from ≈ ₹2 lakh crore in 2014‑15).
05

International Affairs & Organizations

  • Prime Minister Narendra Modi becomes India’s longest‑continuously serving elected Prime Minister (09 Jun 2026).

He has completed 4 399 continuous days, surpassing Jawaharlal Nehru’s 4 398 days.

  • BRICS Agriculture Ministers adopt the “Indore Declaration” (12‑13 Jun 2026).

The declaration emphasizes food security, climate‑resilient agriculture, regenerative farming and digital agriculture; it creates four new BRICS‑wide networks, including a Centre of Excellence on Agro‑Ecology at IIFSR, Modipuram.

  • India‑Tajikistan trade (FY 2025‑26) reaches US$ 58.12 million in exports, a 27.23 % YoY rise.

Key exported items are drug formulations, pulses, industrial machinery and herbal products.

  • Global leaders congratulate PM Modi (09 Jun 2026).

Congratulatory messages came from President Bola Ahmed Tinubu (Nigeria), Prime Minister Anwar Ibrahim (Malaysia), President William Ruto (Kenya), President Ursula von der Leyen (EU) and President Donald Trump (USA), among others.

  • India’s G20 presidency (2026) includes a summit in New Delhi (June 2026).

The agenda focuses on global supply‑chain resilience, climate finance, and Digital Public Infrastructure.

06

Awards, Rankings & Appointments

  • IIT Madras hosts the 3rd BRICS Neuroscience Symposium (05‑07 Jun 2026).

Over 15 international speakers presented the Human Brainstem Atlas, showcasing India’s leadership in high‑resolution brain imaging.

  • National Conference on e‑Governance (Jaipur, 1‑2 Jul 2026) awards Kadepur Gram Panchayat (Maharashtra) the Gold for Digital Transformation.

Kadepur received ₹10 lakh for its AI‑driven service‑delivery platform, the first GP with formal policies on AI, Blockchain, Nanotech, Biotechnology and Robotics.

  • MIFF 2026 (Bengaluru) receives 1 459 film entries from 47 countries.

The festival grants ₹45 lakh across 18 award categories, and presents the V. Shantaram Lifetime Achievement Award.

  • India’s 2026 NIRF ranking places IISc, IIT Madras and St Stephen’s among the top 15 institutions.
  • Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS) publishes IS 20201:2026 – “Community Seed Bank Management – Requirements”.

The standard supports ₹50 lakh one‑time assistance for seed‑bank establishment under the National Food Security & Nutrition Mission.

07

Sports / Environment / Science & Tech

  • India’s men’s Under‑18 Hockey Asia Cup team wins the championship (08 Jun 2026).

They defeated Japan 4‑1; the Ministry of Sports awarded a ₹61.5 lakh cheque to the team, while the women’s team secured bronze and a ₹21 lakh cheque.

  • India launches a 250 MW Solar + Battery Energy Storage System on defence land in Sitapur, UP (09 Jun 2026).

The project, approved by Defence Minister Rajnath Singh, aims to cut long‑term power costs for defence establishments.

  • Global Wind Day 2026 conference in Goa (15 Jun 2026) sets a target of 100 GW wind capacity by 2030.

India added 6.1 GW of wind capacity in FY 2025‑26, the highest annual addition to date.

  • NASA announces the discovery of Olympus Mons on Mars (08 Dec 2023) – referenced in Indian space outreach (June 2026).
  • New AI‑powered insurance product “AI‑Shield” rolls out by a leading Indian fintech (June 2026).

The model uses generative‑AI to price micro‑insurance policies within seconds, reducing underwriting costs by ≈ 30 %.
