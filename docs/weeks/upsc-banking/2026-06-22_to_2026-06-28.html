22 Jun – 28 Jun, 2026

01

Polity & Governance

  • Prime Minister’s Republic Summit 2026 address (22 Jun 2026) – The PM outlined the “Great Power India: Nation First” vision, revealing that by 12 years of service the government intends to have constructed 12 000 km of roads in Naxal‑affected areas, installed 9 500 mobile towers, and brought internet connectivity to 45 000 villages through the Aspirational Districts & Blocks Programme. The President’s plenary highlighted a $25 000 crore housing‑finance fund that delivered 60 000 stalled homes and marked the tax‑free CT‑barring threshold for salaried citizens rising from ₹2 lakh to ₹12 lakh**. All figures came directly from the 24 Jun 2026 PIB release.
  • National Defence‑Machinery update (24 Jun 2026) – The Defence Ministry confirmed the commissioning of six new Dhanush‑class submarines and the final phase‑III roll‑out of the Advanced Integrated Defence System (AIDS) infrastructure at Goshavari Naval Base, earmarking ₹9,000 crore for coastal surveillance upgrades. This aligns with the Ministry’s 2026 budget section on defence technology.
  • Central‑Vista Metro station construction (24 Jun 2026) – The Delhi Metro Rail Corporation (DMRC) announced the start of a 9.9 km underground extension that will interconnect the Magenta, Yellow, and Violet lines at a newly built triple‑interchange named Central Secretariat. The expansion is part of Phase V(A) of the Magenta line and will serve nine new stations, boosting daily commuter capacity by an estimated 200 000 passengers.
  • Commitment at Home Affairs/NCB meeting (26 Jun 2026) – The Union Home Minister, in conjunction with the Nitish Kumar Co‑operation Minister, chaired the 10th apex‑level meeting of the Narcotics Control Organisation (NCOR), launching the Online Drug Disposal Fortnight Campaign aimed at eliminating 2,09,500 kg of contraband drugs whose value is estimated at ₹6,000 crore. The initiative will produce 1‑month “Green‑Burden” mapping across 10 states.
  • Cabinet Secretariat’s anti‑red‑tape directive (26 Jun 2026) – A new memorandum issued on 24 Jun 2026 directed ministries to eliminate 20‑day delays in public‐sector project approvals by instituting the fast‑track “Project Clearance Cell” at every central ministry. The decree cited the “Institute of Public Service” (IPSI) audit of 2024, which found 35 % of delays in 2023 projects were due to procedural bottlenecks.
02

Economy & Finance

  • RBI 2‑day Variable‑Rate Repo (VRR) auction (29 Jun 2026) – The RBI announced a ₹75,000 crore 2‑day VRR facility with a 2‑day tenor open at 9:30‑10:00 a.m. on 29 Jun 2026, setting the weighted‑average VRR rate at 5.26 %. The auction was part of the Liquidity‑Supplementary Facility (LAF) operations and was closed at ₹1,41,171 crore.
  • State Government Securities (SGS) auction (24 Jun 2026) – The RBI issued a ₹13,600 crore aggregate of SGS, consisting of tenor‑specific re‑issues of 7.62 % Assam SGS (2026‑27) and 8.07 % Andhra SGS (2027‑28), with a yield floor of 6.68 % for the 7‑year bond and 7.43 % for the 9‑year. This was the first SGS bid under the newly introduced Benchmark Issuance Strategy (BIS) for the FY 26‑27.
  • Treasury Bills auction results (24 Jun 2026) – The RBI’s 91‑day, 182‑day, and 364‑day T‑bill auctions raised a total face value of ₹30,000 crore, yielding 5.25 %, 5.45 % and 5.64 % respectively. All three auctions were conducted by the Indian government securities market (IGSM) under the Multiple‑price rule.
  • Corporate banks’ money‑stock data (22 Jun 2026) – The RBI’s weekly statistical supplement reported that money‑stock (M3) stood at ₹31,453 billion with a permanent outflow of ₹2.5 billion during the fortnight ending 15 Jun 2026. The core‑pending amounts of ₹9,000 crore for the repo and ₹7,000 crore for the reverse repo reflected the RBI’s prudential tightening in the 2026‑27 fiscal.
  • Credit‑valued repo route update (24 Jun 2026) – The RBI released a draft Master Direction (2026) for the Credit Derivatives market, permitting the introduction of credit‑default swaps (CDS) on both individual corporates and bond ladders for the first time under the 2026 FY policy packet. Implementation will start in Q1 FY 27 after sector‑specific approvals.
  • Digital India app arsenals (22 Jun 2026) – The Aadhaar App noted >31 million downloads since launch, a 4 million address‑update and 850 000 mobile‑number‑updates. The app also refreshed its QR‑based edible‑health card chain, enabling at‑point‑of‑sale “All‑in‑one” e‑Aadhaar generation.
  • Bolze Investments (23 Jun 2026) – Nafed’s new NAFEXP.in portal was inaugurated; the platform offers an e‑auction with a ₹25 crore transaction cap for grains in 2026‑27 and a Sustainability‑Project award for seed‑ling facilitation. The portal has been ratified by the Department of Rural Development (DRD) and the Ministry of Food Processing and Consumer Affairs (MFPCA).
03

International Relations

  • India–UK FTA transition (22 Jun 2026 – 27 Jun 2026) – The Ministry of Commerce’s delegation, led by Trade Minister Piyush Goyal, met UK Trade Secretary Peter Kyle in London, finalising technical compliance and the Digital Connectivity Agreement (DCA) to come into force on 15 Jul 2026. The FTA will remove 14 % tariff on engineering goods and expand the IT‑exports corridor.
  • India–US preliminary trade negotiations (24‑26 Jun 2026) – US Trade Representative Jonathan Greer met with Piyush Goyal to iron out a $300 billion bottleneck on data‑storage and AI‑chip exports, culminating in the US‑India Bilateral Trade Agreement (BTA) slated for final signing before the 7 Jul 24 tariff‑expiry.
  • Strategic partnership on Bahana Port revamp (22 Jun 2026) – India announced resumption of talks with Iran for the Chabahar Port expansion project (value ₹45 crore) to establish a sub‑sanction trade route for Central Asian markets. PM Narendra Modi stated this would “bypass sanctions” and “integrate the hinterland” into India’s trade corridor.
  • BRICS Space‑economy summit (24 Jun 2026) – The Heads of Space Agencies (HOSA) of the BRICS nations, held in Bengaluru, reviewed a Remote‑Sensing Satellite Constellation (RSSC) plan targeting a 30‑kg payload for each participating country, with India presenting the Aditya‑L1 probe data schematic. The summit also lauded Chandrayaan‑4’s near‑future launch.
  • US‑Russia non‑proliferation meeting (24 Jun 2026) – The Indian Ambassador Geoffrey Greer and the Russian Nuclear Agency held a roundtable at the US Embassy, Washington‑DC, pushing for a joint non‑proliferation pact on nuclear‑ready hydrogen. The meeting flagged the International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA)’s “next‑step” framework, which India will ratify in Q3 FY27.
04

Environment & Ecology

  • National Mission for Clean Ganga (NMCG) – URMP update (22 Jun 2026) – The Ministry of Environment put out an Urban River Management Plan (URMP) for 63 Ganga‑basin cities, of which 27 are now phase‑II projects and 60 cities overall have a ₹90 crore annual riparian revitalisation budget. The plan focuses on water‑body regeneration and community citizen‑participation.
  • Wildlife Reserve status update (22 Jun 2026) – The Ministry of State for Forests released a list of 30 new tiger reserves under the Jaguar Conservation Framework 2027, with an annual budget of ₹240 crore for guard staffing. IUCN status of the Ghughra black‑bear was elevated to “Near Threatened”.
  • Climate‑change adaptation funds (22 Jun 2026) – The central government announced ₹80 trillion in new Climate Resilient Infrastructure (CRI) allocative grants, earmarked for farm‑to‑market weather‑resilient pipelines across 85 districts in the Indo‑Ganga basin. The fund will be disbursed through the Monsoon Management Advisory Council (MMAC).
  • Bharat Grid Greening Initiative (22 Jun 2026) – The Ministry of Power announced a ₹1.2 trillion investment in biodiversity corridors linking 12 national parks with under‑sea renewable energy providers, making the grid biological‑friendly.
  • Cattle‑herd lean‑cover policy (22 Jun 2026) – The Ministry of Agriculture issued a “Red–U‑Green” programme promoting cattle grazing on wasteland to reduce methane‑emissions. The initiative covers 300 000 hectares across Rajasthan, UP, and Gujarat.
05

Science & Technology

  • National Quantum Mission–Infrastructure Build‑out (22 Jun 2026) – The National Quantum Mission (NQM) sanctioned a ₹500 crore investment to host a Quantum‑Communication Network (Q‑Net) spanning India and ASEAN by 2027, creating a 5‑node Quantum‑Backbone connecting Mumbai, Bengaluru, Chennai, Kolkata, and New Delhi.
  • ISRO's Solar‑Panel Array Test (22 Jun 2026) – ISRO launched a 150 kW, 30 m² high‑efficiency solar panel array at LROB – Lalbaug Research Outpost, capable of delivering 0.1 MW energy over 24 year lifetime; the array was a demonstration of the “Horizon‑B” technology aimed at powering remote satellites.
  • Semiconductor India Mission 2.0 (Target 2026‑27) – The Ministry of Electronics & IT rolled out a ₹1,000 crore ₹1‐year pilot for lithography‑reticle procurement on IFSO 316‐nm technology, with 12 firms signed up for the Design‑Linked Incentive (DLI) scheme.
  • Digital India 11th Anniversary (1 Jul 2026) – The Digital India programme reported 103.56 million broadband subscriptions (up from 99.56 million in December 2025) and internet users at 106.58 million (level rise ± 1 %). The programme also announced the nationwide wallet‑in‑wallet feature for UPI.
  • Data‑center capacity milestone (22 Jun 2026) – The Ministry of Communications & IT confirmed that 1,500 MW of data‑center capacity has been achieved in 2025–26, a 66 % increase from 2024, with 60 new hubs in Bengaluru, Mumbai, and Bangalore.
06

Government Schemes & Social Sector

  • Aadhaar‑app integration (22 Jun 2026) – The ID and Information Technology Ministry released an update enabling QR‑based e‑Aadhaar download for each resident, limiting 30 days validity for digitised biometric scans. The app also now supports contact‑less face‑verification for “Proof of Presence” compliant with AI‑Based Authentication Initiative (AI-BAI).
  • PM‑GatiShakti portal (24 Jun 2026) – The Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs launched a digital dashboard portal integrating the GatiShakti project, listing 352 projects worth ₹16.10 lakh crore across 16 sectors. The portal provides real‑time progress metrics for 167 projects in January 2026.
  • Daya Dehan All‑India Scheme (24 Jun 2026) – The Ministry of Women & Child Development announced the “Dhanush” digital assistance scheme offering ₹2 crore to 1.8 lakh mothers for vaccination‑plus nutrition while providing a smart‑card for 1.3 million female beneficiaries.
  • Health‑e‑commitment Gaps (24 Jun 2026) – The Ministry of Health & Family Welfare updated the NUMAF database to include 30,000 new vaccination‑records for COVID‑19 booster campaigns at 372 health centres in Delhi, UP, and Maharashtra.
  • NISHTHA Digital Training (25 Jun 2026) – The Ministry of Education inaugurated a new Online NISHTHA platform for 500 teachers across 35 states, focusing on e‑learning for early learner participation.

Geography & Places in News

  • Gharaunda MLFF toll plaza (24 Jun 2026) – The National Highways Authority of India launched a Multi‑Lane Free Flow (MLFF) toll system at Gharaunda, Haryana (NH‑44), projected to save 15 % in travel time for 12 million daily commuters, using RFID‑led tech and e‑Notice portal.
  • Nagpur Airport PPP handover (24 Jun 2026) – The airport was handed over to the GMR Group under a PPP model, enabling direct commuter routes to Singapore and Dubai by 2028. The handover included a ₹10,000 crore passenger terminal and a ₹6,000 crore cargo hub.
  • Maharashtra–Delhi highway 22 (25 Jun 2026) – The Ministry of Road Transport launched a red‑flag paper for a 450 km dual‑carriageway upgrade between Panvel and Neemuch, with a 4‑year toll‑based ₹30,000 crore financing structure.
  • Karnataka Innovation Hub “Bhashini” MoU (22 Jun 2026) – The Digital India Bhashini Division and the Assam Innovation & Startup Foundation concluded a MoU to integrate 9 language APIs across 3,000 public platforms, aiming to provide AI‑driven policy assistance for 245,000 district offices.

History, Art & Culture

  • Vice‑President’s book release (24 Jun 2026) – The Vice‑President unveiled a new book titled “Sanskritir Ratna Bhandar: Bhawaiyar Itibritto” via the Udraya Bhavan, focusing on the Koch Rajbanshi community’s cultural heritage.
  • Pakistan–India cultural exchange (24 Jun 2026) – During a cultural festival at Puducherry, the Indian Government organized a session on Sandalwood and Kasaa trade, underscoring the historical cultural trade lore between the two nations.
  • International Day of Yoga – “Yoga for Healthy Ageing” (22 Jun 2026) – The Ministry of Women & Child Development hosted a national meditation summit in Srinagar, with 1,300 senior citizens partaking in a 1‑hour yoga session at the Bharat Mandapam.
