Polity & Governance

  • Vice‑President C.P. Radhakrishnan’s briefing on the National Cadet Corps (NCC) and Sainik Schools (13 Jul 2026) – He urged states to intensify infrastructure and land support for NCC, aiming to up‑skill about 20 lakh youths and to increase intake capacity of 3‑5 Sainik schools per state, which strengthens grassroots defence‑civic nexus.
  • The National Conference on Next‑Gen Administrative and e‑Governance Reforms (13 Jul 2026) – Conducted in Meghalaya, the conference removed nearly 2,000 obsolete rules in the past decade and set a “Reform Express” that will incorporate AI, cybersecurity and DRM across 56 crore Jan Dhan accounts, illustrating the government’s digital‑inclusive agenda.
  • High‑level review meeting between Union Agriculture Minister Shivraj Singh Chouhan and Chief Minister Suvendu Adhikari of West Bengal (14 Jul 2026) – Approved 19 46‑crore ₹ infrastructure projects and 8,508 crore ₹ rural development package under VB‑G RAM G, reinforcing the Centre–state growth partnership, while earmarking 295 crore ₹ for women SHG credit – a flagship scheme under PM‑AAY G.
  • Prime Minister’s Insight on Trade‑Related Digital Frameworks (15 Jul 2026) – PM Modi highlighted the requirement of CBIC‑issued Unique Reference Numbers for UK‑India CETA duty benefits, implying a shift to digital trade compliance under the India‑UK Comprehensive Economic and Trade Agreement (effective 15 Jul 2026).
  • Eighth Pay Commission’s Significant Gazette Provisions (13 Jul 2026) – The Commission’s 6‑point list extends allowances, pension, health‐insurance, and new performance incentive frameworks to central employees and pensioners, altering the civil‑service wage structure for the forthcoming fiscal year.
02

Economy & Finance

  • India–UK CETA Effective Date and Compliance (15 Jul 2026) – The pact entered force on 15 Jul 2026, granting near‑zero duty for 99 % of Indian exports and enforcing CBIC digital authentication of export declarations, a critical test of preferential trade execution.
  • Variable‑Rate Repo (VRR) Auctions (17 Jul 2026) – Two auctions for 3‑day tenure at 5.26 % weighted and 7‑day tenure at ₹125 crore notified salary; RBI’s short‑term liquidity injections reflect the bank’s policy stance amid bullish March–April inflation.
  • State Government Securities (Securities) 14 Jul 2026 – Andhra, Gujarat, Maharashtra, Meghalaya, Mizoram, Punjab, Rajasthan, Tamil Nadu and Telangana raised ₹24.8 crore ₹ districtwise, with cut‑off yields ranging 7.03–7.78 %, exemplifying state fiscal strengthening against the national deficit.
  • Micro‑, Small‑ and Medium‑Enterprise (MSME) Digital Service – Udyam Registration Portal (14 Jul 2026) – The portal facilitated over 8.9 crore enterprise registrations, supporting the cadre of 38 crore employees through formalised digital compliance, echoing the department’s “Digital India” reform agenda.
  • External Commercial Borrowings (ECB) & Foreign Currency Borrowings (FCNR(B)) (13 Jul 2026) – RBI declared a par‑dated USD‑Rupee swap, extending ECB and OFCB eligibility till 31 Dec 2026, underscoring the RBI’s proactive stance to manage foreign‑exchange risk.
03

International Relations

  • India‑UK CETA (15 Jul 2026) – The free trade agreement now grants duty‑free access to 99 % of Indian exports, and requires CBIC‑issued UTRs for export declarations, positioning India as a major UK supplier and enhancing bilateral trade ties.
  • India–EU Trade Pact Finalizing Legal Scrutiny (15 Jul 2026) – The EU treaty is near conclusion, promising 93 % duty‑free entry for Indian goods, signifying cosmopolitan supply‑chain integration and a move to an emerging partnership.
  • India–Oman Trade Data (13 Jul 2026) – The protocol on tariff concession requests mandates a structured procedural checklist, enabling smoother access for Indian exporters of dates, marble, & packaging to Omani waters.
  • India–Finland Trade Visit (16 Jul 2026) – Minister Piyush Goyal’s meeting with Nokia, VTT, KONE and Kemppi signalled tech and power‑sector co‑development, reinforcing Europe‑India collaboration on industrial digitalisation and clean tech.
  • India–Russia INNOPROM Fair (13 Jul 2026) – The industrial fair slated for September 9‑11 will host pharmaceutical and chemical showcases, widening commercial channels with the Russian bloc and broadening India’s defence‑industrial outreach.

Environment & Ecology

  • Catch the Rain 2026 Water‑Conservation Campaign (13‑19 Jul 2026) – The Ministry of Jal Shakti accelerated the programme, targeting 1.5 crore recharge structures, 90 kha water bodies and 12 kha salvage schemes, reflecting a nation‑wide shift to rainwater harvesting and groundwater recharge.
  • National Ecology Area Development – NECTAR Geo‑Informatics (14 Jul 2026) – The NECTAR center in Shillong now has 13 Weather radars and 171 seismological stations, underlining India’s capacity to monitor monsoon intensity, earthquake early warning, and climate‑driven hazard mitigation.
  • Ultra‑Sensitive Ammonia Sensor – Development by CeNS (14 Jul 2026) – The VOx/VS₂ heterostructure sensor achieves a 319 ppb detection limit at room temperature, demonstrating the nano‑tech strides to curtail industrial emissions and bolster environmental compliance.
  • India’s First Indigenous Hydrogen‑Fuel‑Cell Train (16 Jul 2026) – The 10‑coach train (2,600 passengers) uses planar PEM fuel cells, 3 kW per DPC, with 50 bar compressed hydrogen found at Jind’s 3,000 kg storage plant, signalling clean‑mobility revolution.
  • National Infrastructure – 6‑Lane Greenfield Corridor (15 Jul 2026) – A ₹14,447.64 crore corridor with 910 m cable‑stayed bridge reduces travel time by 50 % and fosters economic corridors across Uttar Pradesh, showcasing eco‑infrastructure synergy.

Science & Technology

  • Space‑Based Applications for the Northeast – GEO‑spatial Mapping (14 Jul 2026) – The Ministry launched ~130 space‑based projects, of which 50 remain ongoing, to map agriculture, disaster management and resource planning across the six NE states, demonstrating India’s advanced remote sensing capacity.
  • INSPACe Programme 2023 – Private Space Push (15 Jul 2026) – Launch of 400 new space start‑ups (from 1 in 2014) and a 4,500‑organisation DBE registry, paving the way for 70 % domestic participation in launch vehicle manufacturing and satellite components under the new policy.
  • ISRO’s Novel Titan Sensor – Ultra‑Sensitive Ammonia Sensing (14 Jul 2026) – The heterostructure sensor, detailed in ACS Sensors (DOI 10.1021/acssensors.5c02600), achieves sub‑PPM detection at room temperature, indicating a breakthrough in environmental monitoring.
  • MoSCKR – Ultra‑Sensitive Ammonia Sensor (14 Jul 2026) – The sensor’s mobile prototype and self‑powered design demonstrate Indian R&D’s push toward low‑cost, high‑accuracy industrial safety tools.
  • VIKRAM‑1 – India’s First Privately Deployable Orbital Launch Vehicle (15 Jul 2026) – By Skyroot, it carries 350 kg payloads into LEO and signals a democratization of space access, positioning India at a corner of the global launch‑ers list.
